About

A Little About Me

Growing up in a single parent household, where my mom raised my brother and me on her own, wasn't easy. We had to move to America for better opportunities, witnessing the burden she had to carry, I knew I had to grow up fast. From a young age, I had started working, learning to adapt, along with communicating with people and figuring things out on the fly.

My interest in tech started the way a lot of people's does: watching shows where "hackers" would break into systems and thinking, wait, how does that actually work? That curiosity never left me. It pushed me to stop watching but instead taking action. My first real attempt was breaking into my high school's network (with permission) and it worked. From that experience, I learned that I was passionate about cybersecurity and computer science.

I decided to pursue a career in cybersecurity and computer science, where I made my first approach by securing a position known as SWAT Tech at Virginia Tech. Which is an entry-level technician role where I help students troubleshoot technical issues, repair equipment, and loan out devices like computers and peripherals. Outside of that, I'm actively pursuing certifications like CompTIA Security+ and competiting in active competitions. I'm building towards a career where I can do this at higher levels - whether that's through an internship, a SOC analyst role, or security engineer. But beyond this, my goal/wish at the end is to give back my mom the life she deserves and to release the burden she carried.
